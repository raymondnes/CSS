First is Ambition

"how can I lose when I came here with nothing?" First is ambition. By all means, punch above your weight. Ride life with laughter and style. Earn the respect of those you admire. Try your hands at wit and wry humor. Play with ambition and pursue mastery.

ARTICLE DEMO

Memories

Covet the best memories and give amazing sex. Apply yourself to positions that would naturally be beyond you; test your mettle. Leave the cocoon of inertia. Try to try. If nothing matters, at least dignify the process. Technically you cannot lose.

Second is Contentment

"How can I lose when I came here with nothing?" Second is Contentment. All of Life is suffering - this is Budha's first 'Noble Truth'. The source of this suffering is or endless desire . So by all means, play also with contentment. Play with Kids, as you do adults.

No External Pressure

Savor the food you bite. Don't take yourself too seriously; you're not all that. No one is. Try to make others happy; life is not all about you. Have at least one aspect of your life you fully control - no external pressure - and take full responsibility for the outcomes

Call home

Accept opportunities for growth, but don't be in a hurry to grow up. Reach for the skies, but try not to lose sight of things larger than self. Yes, ambition is necessary, but ambition does not aways have to be "grand". And oh, call home; family is way cooler than you think.

Nothing Is Enough

We take only to let go. We live only to die; this is the tectoniv structure of the universe. Nothing is enough. The "best" is only an ideal again which we measure the material world. There will always be more positions to apply to, greater sex, wittier people, more ambitions

A form of Death Itself

This is the ultimate paradox. I want to be great yet what value is that if life is all vanity? But no, we can be museums of ambition and contentment. We can discern whrn to fly and when to perch. Life without both is painfully empty; A form of death itself

Discernment

How do we know when to draw the line? Of God I ask for discernment. My mortal intellect is insufficient. I believe that everything is made of matter. And that there's a 'matter' behind that matter, that truly matters. Maybe that's God. I believe he hears me. I can't make sense of it any other way

Acceptance

"How can I lose when I came here with nothing?" Acceptance. We can unconditionally accept all of life and love it; the highs, the lows, the meaning, the meaninglessness, the melody, the melodilessness, the pain and suffering. Actually, until we die, we have no choice. We're all in.
